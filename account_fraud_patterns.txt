ACCOUNT-RELATED FRAUD PATTERNS

ACCOUNT TAKEOVER PATTERNS:
- Sudden change of contact information followed by large transfers
- Multiple failed login attempts from unfamiliar locations
- Account accessed from IP addresses in different countries within short timeframes
- Unusual changes to direct deposit information
- Unfamiliar devices accessing accounts
- Session hijacking of active banking sessions
- SIM swapping to intercept 2FA codes

APPLICATION FRAUD PATTERNS:
- Synthetic identities combining real and fake information
- Identity theft with slight personal information modifications
- Rapid succession of loan applications across multiple institutions
- Income documentation inconsistencies
- Address mismatches between application documents
- Fake employment documentation
- Hidden debt obligations not disclosed

INTERNAL FRAUD PATTERNS:
- Employee overriding normal verification procedures
- After-hours system access without business justification
- Modified customer records by authorized users
- Approving fraudulent loans to accomplices
- Creating fake vendor accounts for payments
